Buckets — Help & FAQ

What is a bucket?

A bucket is a named "job" for your money — rent, groceries, the car fund, a vacation goal. Every dollar in your budget account is either sitting in Ready to Spend (unassigned) or assigned to a bucket. That's zero-based budgeting: nothing is left to chance.

Each bucket has an Allocation (the money you've actually put in it this month), and usually a Target/Budget (what it's supposed to cost). The gap between the two drives the bucket's status pill (e.g. "Funding — $50 short", "Funded", "Overspent $20").

Bucket types

Expense (default)

A normal monthly category — groceries, gas, subscriptions. Each month its allocation resets: whatever you didn't spend goes back to Ready to Spend automatically. It doesn't carry over unless you turn on Rollover.

Example: "Groceries" has a $400 target. You allocate $400 this month, spend $350. Next month, the leftover $50 returns to Ready to Spend — "Groceries" starts fresh at $0 allocated again.

Expense with Rollover

Same as Expense, but unspent money stays in the bucket instead of returning to Ready to Spend — an envelope that carries a balance forward.

Example: "Car Maintenance" has Rollover on, target $50/month. You allocate $50 and spend $0 in January. In February the bucket still shows $50 available, plus whatever you allocate that month — so a $300 repair in March is already covered.

Vault

A long-term holding bucket that accumulates across every month — for money you're setting aside but don't have a specific target/date for yet (an emergency fund, a general savings cushion).

Example: You allocate $100/month to "Emergency Fund" (a Vault). After 6 months it shows a total of $600, even though no single month's allocation was $600 — Vaults remember everything you've ever put in.

Sinking Fund / Goal

Like a Vault (accumulates over time), but with a Target Amount and optional Target Date. Shows a progress bar and tells you exactly how much to save per month to hit the goal on time.

Example: "New Laptop" — target $1,200 by December (8 months away). The bucket shows "Save $150/month to reach your goal" and a progress bar that fills as you contribute.

Flex

For categories that genuinely vary month to month and don't have a fixed target — "Dining Out," "Misc." Flex buckets don't show "over/under budget" against a target; instead they just track what you've spent vs. allocated, with a simple "Cover $X" prompt if spending exceeds the allocation. Flex buckets are excluded from the Funding Rate metric in Reports, since they're not meant to hit a fixed number.

Example: "Dining Out" (Flex) — you allocate $150. You spend $180. The bucket shows "Cover $30" — a nudge to move $30 from somewhere else, but it's not flagged as "over budget" the way a fixed-target bucket would be.

Allocation vs. Target/Budget

Target/Budget is what a bucket is supposed to cost — set it once in the bucket's settings (it carries forward to future months automatically when set from the current month).

Allocation is the actual money you've put into the bucket this month — set via the + / − buttons or the alloc field on each bucket row, or in bulk via Distribute.

Example: "Internet" target is $80. At the start of the month you allocate $80 → pill shows "Funded". If you only allocate $60, the pill shows "Funding — $20 short" until you top it up.

Status pills explained
  • Overspent $X — spending exceeded the bucket's target this month.
  • Funding — $X short — allocation hasn't reached the target yet.
  • Unfunded — has a target but $0 allocated.
  • Funded — allocation meets or exceeds the target.
  • Paid — funded and the full target has already been spent.
  • Overfunded — you've allocated more than the target (extra is "parked" in the bucket).
  • Open — an envelope bucket with no target, nothing allocated yet.
  • ✓ Handled — manually marked done for this month (see below).
"Handled" — what it does

Sometimes you allocate more than you end up spending — and you don't want to leave that extra money sitting idle in the bucket, or have the bucket nag you with "$100 short" forever because the target is higher than what you actually needed this month.

Marking a bucket Handled does two things, for the current month only:

  1. Releases any unspent allocation back to Ready to Spend (allocation drops to match what was actually spent).
  2. Treats the bucket's target as "met" for this month — every report, the funding rate, and the bucket's pill all show it as fully funded (✓ Handled), so it stops showing a misleading "short" amount.

Example: "Rent" has a $2,200 target. This month rent was actually $2,100 (a discount). You allocated $2,200. Mark it Handled: allocation drops to $2,100, the freed-up $100 returns to Ready to Spend, and Reports show Rent as 100% funded this month — not "$100 short."

Toggling Handled back off just removes the badge — it does not put the $100 back into the bucket (that money has already been freed for you to reassign).

Distribute (assigning Ready to Spend)

Click DISTRIBUTE RTS → in the sidebar to open a modal that suggests how to spread your unassigned cash across buckets that still need funding — typically prioritizing buckets that are unfunded or short first. Check/uncheck buckets and adjust amounts, then apply.

Example: You get paid and have $500 in Ready to Spend. Distribute shows "Groceries — $50 short", "Internet — $80 short", "Emergency Fund — flex $370". Apply it, and all three allocations update in one step.

Vault Transfer & Vault Release

Vault Transfer moves money from one Vault/Sinking/Goal bucket directly into another — no need to release to Ready to Spend and re-allocate.

Example: You decide your "Vacation" goal is fully funded and want to redirect future contributions toward "New Car" — transfer the accumulated balance directly.

Vault Release moves accumulated money out of a Vault back into Ready to Spend, for when plans change and you want that cash freed up for something else.

Example: "New Laptop" goal has $600 saved, but you've decided to use your old laptop another year — Release sends that $600 back to Ready to Spend to reassign anywhere.

Categories & ordering

Buckets are grouped into categories (Housing, Food, Subscriptions, etc.) shown as collapsible cards. Each category card shows a progress bar of allocated / target across all its buckets. Add a new bucket directly from the "+ New bucket in…" strip at the bottom of any category, or use the global add strip to pick a category explicitly.

Future months & planning

The month tabs at the top let you peek at upcoming months. Future months always show today's targets — if you change a bucket's target now, every future month immediately reflects it (no need to update each month individually). Allocations you make to a future month are "pre-claimed" and subtracted from today's Ready to Spend, so you can't accidentally double-spend money you've already earmarked for next month's rent.

Closing the month & archiving

Close Month locks in the month's numbers for historical reporting — you can Reopen it later if you need to make corrections. Archive a bucket (from its settings) when you no longer need it — it disappears from the active list but its history is preserved for past-month reports.
